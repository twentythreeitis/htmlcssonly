Abel makkonen tesfaye

Canadian singer, songwriter, and record producer.
Scarborough, Toronto, Ontario, Canada

The Weeknd, born Abel Makkonen Tesfaye on February 16, 1990, is a Canadian singer, songwriter, and record producer who has left an indelible mark on the music industry. Raised in Toronto, he gained widespread recognition with his enigmatic R&B-infused sound and captivating vocals. The Weeknd's journey began by anonymously uploading his music to YouTube in 2010, garnering attention for his uniquely seductive blend of soulful melodies and haunting lyricism. His debut mixtapes, "House of Balloons," "Thursday," and "Echoes of Silence," collectively known as the "Trilogy," propelled him into the limelight, earning critical acclaim for their dark, introspective themes.

In 2013, The Weeknd released his first studio album, "Kiss Land," which further solidified his position in the music scene. However, it was his 2015 album "Beauty Behind the Madness" that catapulted him to superstardom. The album's chart-topping single "Can't Feel My Face" became a global anthem, showcasing his ability to craft catchy pop-infused hits without compromising his signature mystique. Subsequent albums like "Starboy" (2016) and "After Hours" (2020) continued to showcase his evolution as an artist, experimenting with various genres while maintaining his distinctive essence.

Beyond his musical prowess, The Weeknd's impact extends to his visually striking music videos and memorable live performances, including his halftime show at the 2021 Super Bowl. With numerous awards, including Grammys, under his belt, The Weeknd's influence on contemporary music remains profound, as he continually pushes boundaries and redefines the sonic landscape.
